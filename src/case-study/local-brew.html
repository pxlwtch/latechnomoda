Local Brew is an iOS app created for a Hackathon at Coding Dojo in San Jose, CA.

This project was created for a weekend hackathon, our task was to make an iOS game using spritekit and Swift. Since this was week one of our Swift learning, the hardest part about making this game was making the game work.

Audience
Our audience was our cohort and the judges, who were guest programmers who worked in tech.
We were judged on
  • implementation of spritekit
  • UI
  • game design
  • presentation
Our targets were young 20-something year olds, people who would play games on their phone to pass the time such as Candy Crush.
Design Process

The task was to create a functioning game so we opted to make the game as simple as possible, users played against a timer, and each time a circle fell inside the us a point was tallied. At first we planned to make the rings go through the sticks, but then we realized we didn't know how to do that in Swift so I changed it into the u shaped 'baskets' which then became u shaped pool noodles to go with the water theme.

Constraints

As usual, time was a big constraint. This was a weekend hackathon at my bootcamp, and our team had pretty much been strangers before the hackathon so bad communication (and lack of) also had a negative role in our experience.

Our research consisted of asking our cohort mates (who were also working on their hackathon project) what they thought about the game. There was really good suggestions that led to the final design.

In Perspective

Aqua was successful! It placed us in second place, out of twelve, and we got to learn how to use Spritekit!


We prioritized making the bubbles work a bit too much, in retrospective, that was time that would have been better put to use in other aspects such as the physics. Given more time we could have developed this into a much more exciting game, but it really played like the original.
